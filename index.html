The World Cryptologic Competition (WCC) is a worldwide open competition. The WCC itself is based in the United States. The WCC 2023 is the WCC's first year.

In most ways similar to the AES competition in 2001 (the "Advanced Encryption Standard process"), the World Cryptologic Competition looks to produce real value by production of innovative new algorithms, results, methods, and products (such as new cryptocurrencies).

The WCC 2023 has 3 entry categories: Category A (Block Cipers), Category B (Digest Functions), and Category C (Stream Ciphers). In future years there will also be categories for analysis and invention- and perhaps others as well.

Competition Format

Technologies

For reasons of expedience, this year the WCC will use GitHub and E-Mail for all official communication purposes. In future years we will have private hosting, E-Mail lists, chat rooms, and access to a reference machine, in addition to GitHub.

WCC 2023 is announced to (how many?) mailing lists, (how many?) colleges & universities, the ACM (Association for Computing Machinery), the AMS (American Mathematical Society), the International Association for Cryptologic Research (IACR), the U.S. National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST), the U.S. National Security Administration (NSA), the U.K. Government Communications Headquarters (GCHQ), the Japanese National center of Incident readiness and Strategy For Cybersecurity (NISC), the Israeli National Cyber Directorate, and similar organizations around the world.

Language

All language used in the official discussion and official entry materials must be English.

Qualification

The WCC is worldwide and open to all entrants, even anonymous ones. For entrants, there is a 10$ USD entry fee per entry. If you are a member of an institution such as a school, your institution may be able to to pay your entry fee. There is no fee to be a judge. The entry fees help support the WCC and avoid disingenuous entries. If you do not want to pay the entry fee, you can request a fee waiver in your application E-Mail, just tell us why.

Scoring

Scoring will be done on a 1-10 scale, in whole numbers, in 4 "files".

  1. 1) Security (Most Important)
  2. 2) Innovation
  3. 3) Speed
  4. 4) Aesthetics
Only in the case of a tie (equal averge score by all Judges) will the following file be considered. In the event of a full tie, first, it will be checked if the tie can be broken by only including scores from Distinguished Judges. If so, then that will be the result. If not, then the Judges will be asked to re-score. If the tie cannot be broken, then both entries will pass.

Judges

WCC Judges are experts from academia and industry. The minimum requirements to be a judge is any of: An academic degree (MS or PhD) with a crypto-related specialization, published crypto-related academic work in a peer-reviewed setting, or well-known and studied open-source work in crypto.

In WCC 2023 there are two classes of judge: "Judge", and "Distinguished Judge". There is no difference between the weight of vote from the two classes; but in the event of a true tie; if it is possible to break the tie by only counting votes from Distinguished Judges, then that will be done. In addition, Distinguished Judges are awarded more for their work. Examples of someone who would qualify to be a "Distinguished Judge" would be Kaisa Nyberg, Ron Rivest, Vincent Rijmen, John Kelsey, Don Coppersmith, or Bruce Schneier. Distinguished Judges, if any, will be chosen from the pool of Judges after the registration/submissions phase ends (Phase 1).
